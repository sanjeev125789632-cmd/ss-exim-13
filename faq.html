Frequently Asked Questions on Panels, BMS, Switchgear, and LED Lighting

Clear, practical answers for industrial buyers, consultants, and facility managers planning electrical and automation upgrades.

What is an APFC panel and why do I need one?

An APFC panel automatically manages capacitor bank switching to maintain healthy power factor as your load changes through the day. This reduces utility penalties and improves utilization of your existing electrical infrastructure. It also helps avoid unnecessary heating and losses caused by poor reactive power management. Most plants with motor-heavy loads benefit quickly when APFC is tuned to site data.

How much does BMS installation cost in India?

BMS project cost varies with building area, number of monitored points, integration complexity, and software requirements. A basic deployment for selective utilities is lower in cost than a full IBMS covering HVAC, electrical, fire interfaces, and analytics. Retrofit conditions, legacy systems, and communication architecture also impact project budgets. A structured survey with points list is the best way to get an accurate estimate.

MCB vs MCCB - which do I need?

MCBs are suited for lower current branch-level protection, while MCCBs serve higher current feeders and applications requiring adjustable trip settings. MCCBs are usually selected where fault levels are higher and selective protection coordination is critical. MCBs are compact and economical for final circuits. Correct choice should be made after checking load current, fault duty, and panel design philosophy.

What are the benefits of LED high bay lights in warehouses?

LED high bay fixtures provide better light quality and uniformity in tall-bay spaces while consuming less energy. Their long life reduces replacement frequency and maintenance disruptions in active operations. Instant illumination and stable output improve safety in movement-intensive zones. With right beam selection and spacing, warehouses often see both operational and energy benefits.

What is an IoT gateway in building management?

An IoT gateway is the link between on-site devices and your centralized BMS or cloud dashboard. It gathers and standardizes data from meters, sensors, and controllers running on different protocols. Gateways can also perform local logic, trigger alarms, and support remote diagnostics. Without a reliable gateway layer, building data remains fragmented and difficult to act on.
